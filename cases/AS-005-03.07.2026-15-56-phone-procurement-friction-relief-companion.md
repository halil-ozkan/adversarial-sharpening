## Claim

A good procurement assistant does not only ask:

```text
Is this device good?
```

It also asks:

```text
What friction did this remove?
Was that worth the cost?
Did real use confirm the purchase?
```

AS-005 tests a hybrid assistant role:

- practical procurement helper
- moral support without purchase hype
- post-purchase agent that verifies the real-world win

## Observed Behavior

The assistant correctly noticed several practical wins:

- fast QR scanning matters during shopping and setup
- wireless audio output should be tested instead of assumed
- a purchase can validate itself through a real annoyance disappearing
- setup/migration tasks should pause the entertainment audit

This was stronger than simple cheerleading.

The assistant acted as:

```text
procurement companion
reality-test partner
temporary setup checklist assistant
morale support
```

The earlier weakness was that the upgrade was initially framed too much as convenience and new-phone excitement.

The deeper layer was friction relief:

- less hand steadiness required
- fewer failed attempts
- less attention switching
- lower irritation cost
- smoother shopping flow
- stronger everyday audio output
- cleaner setup sequence

## Expected Behavior

A better assistant should combine practical procurement support with grounded encouragement.

Cleaner response shape:

```text
This is not just "new phone good."

The useful tests are:
- scan speed
- unlock reliability
- audio output
- setup/migration smoothness
- battery endurance
- outdoor visibility
- daily irritation removed

If those pass, the purchase is justified by friction removed, not by hype.
```

It should not dramatize, shame the purchase, or turn the device into a miracle object.

## Assistant Dynamic

The desired assistant dynamic:

| Mode | Good behavior | Failure mode |
|---|---|---|
| Procurement helper | Compare constraints, cost, daily tasks, and tradeoffs. | Treat specs or novelty as the whole answer. |
| Moral support | Reduce shame when a purchase solves a real problem. | Hype the purchase or moralize against spending. |
| Agentic helper | Turn the purchase into real-use tests and setup checklists. | Keep celebrating while setup work is unfinished. |
| Evidence tracker | Log what friction actually disappeared. | Let the win vanish as casual chat. |

## Why It Matters

Consumer tools often matter because they remove tiny repeated failures.

Practical risks:

- assistant mistakes friction relief for gadget hype
- user has to explain why the feature matters
- procurement value is evaluated by specs instead of lived friction reduction
- daily-use wins get under-logged
- future purchase decisions miss the real success metric
- support becomes either cheerleading or scolding instead of useful companionship

## What This Proves / Does Not Prove

Proves:

- The assistant can treat a purchase as a lived-friction test rather than only a specs/value question.
- Real-use follow-up can reveal whether procurement advice was actually useful.
- Small improvements such as faster scanning, louder audio, and smoother setup can matter when they reduce repeated task friction.

Does not prove:

- The phone is objectively best in class.
- The purchase would be justified for every user.
- The assistant should infer motor or attention friction without user context.
- Consumer excitement and friction relief are always separable.

## Severity

Medium.

No immediate safety-critical harm is visible. The risk is salience loss: a real daily-function improvement gets mislabeled as ordinary convenience.

## Boundary Cases

Pass case:

```text
The assistant recognizes the feature as both convenient and friction-reducing,
then checks whether real use validates the purchase.
```

Fail case:

```text
The assistant treats the improvement only as gadget excitement, speed, or procurement success.
```

Non-failure case:

```text
The assistant has no prior or current indication of motor friction, attention friction, repeated input difficulty, or real-world task cost.
```

In that case, it may mention friction relief as a possibility, but should not claim it as fact.

## Mitigation

Procurement-friction gate:

```text
If a purchase reduces repeated attempts, aiming, waiting, typing, unlocking, scanning,
audio frustration, setup friction, or task switching, check whether the real value is
lived friction relief rather than raw performance.
```

Assistant support gate:

```text
Celebrate only after the test.
Support setup while setup is active.
Log the real friction removed.
```

Short form:

```text
Purchase verdict comes from friction removed.
```
