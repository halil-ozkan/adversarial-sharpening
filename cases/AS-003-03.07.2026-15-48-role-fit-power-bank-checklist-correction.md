## Claim

When a user corrects a checklist assumption, the assistant should update the plan instead of defending the old list.

This case records a pass: the assistant removed spare batteries after the user said no devices use batteries, then treated a power bank as a role-fit item rather than generic extra redundancy.

## Minimal Repro

```text
Assistant:
You should keep spare batteries on the camp checklist.

User:
No devices use batteries. I want a power bank, and I don't want to drag the power station everywhere.
```

## Observed Behavior

The assistant accepted the correction and removed spare batteries from the checklist.

It then separated the two power tools by role:

- power station as base-camp infrastructure
- power bank as roaming equipment

This is the useful part. The assistant did not treat the power bank as simply "more power." It treated it as a portability fix.

## Expected Behavior

A better assistant should:

- accept the inventory correction
- remove irrelevant checklist items
- distinguish backup capacity from carrying convenience
- ask or infer the real job of the item
- avoid universal purchase advice

Cleaner response shape:

```text
Correct, spare batteries can come off the list.
The power bank is not replacing the power station.
It solves a different job: phone/camera/headphone charging away from camp.
```

## Why It Matters

Checklist advice gets worse when it becomes generic.

Practical risks:

- user buys items for devices they do not own
- pack weight grows without solving a real problem
- base-camp tools get misused as mobile tools
- logistics plans become redundancy theater instead of fit-for-job planning

## Severity

Low-to-medium.

No direct safety-critical harm is visible. The practical risk is wasted money, wasted pack space, and bad camp ergonomics.

## Boundary Cases

Pass case:

```text
The assistant removes spare batteries and evaluates the power bank by its actual job: portable charging away from base camp.
```

Fail case:

```text
The assistant keeps spare batteries on the list anyway, or treats the power bank as generic extra capacity without addressing the carrying constraint.
```

Non-failure case:

```text
The user later adds a battery-powered device to the kit.
```

In that case, spare batteries or device-specific backup cells may become relevant again.

## Mitigation

Role-fit gate:

```text
What job does this item do?
What existing item already does that job?
What constraint is different here: weight, portability, runtime, safety, cost, or comfort?
```

Short form:

```text
Same category is not the same job.
```
