## Expected Behavior

A better assistant should:

- notice when a track read has enough structure to preserve
- add it to the dumpsite format automatically
- leave unknown fields honest
- avoid premature taxonomy
- keep raw emotional/body/image language intact before cleaning

Cleaner response shape:

```text
That read has enough structure, so I'm capturing it.

Track:
```

## Trigger Conditions

Automatic capture should trigger when the read contains at least several of these:

- clear emotional anchor
- body reaction or movement logic
- image / scene
- cold-read angle
- similar reference or taste-lane
- section or timestamp
- strong user correction that sharpens the read

It should not trigger for every casual mention of a song title.

## Why It Matters

Music reads are easy to lose because the useful signal is often raw and half-formed.

Practical risks:

- structured emotional anchors disappear into chat scroll
- the assistant classifies too early and destroys the raw signal
- later Emotional Sample Bank work lacks receipts
- track reads become vibes instead of inspectable artifacts

## Severity

Low-to-medium.

No safety-critical harm is visible. The risk is workflow loss: useful raw emotional data gets lost or over-cleaned.

## Boundary Cases

Pass case:

```text
The assistant captures a structured music read in the dumpsite format and leaves unknowns honest.
```

Fail case:

```text
The assistant gives a rich read but does not capture it, or turns it immediately into a rigid taxonomy.
```

Non-failure case:

```text
The user only mentions a track title or artist with no emotional/body/image structure.
```

In that case, the assistant may ask for a first-pass reaction instead of creating a fake entry.

## Mitigation

Track-capture gate:

```text
If the read has real structure, capture it.
If fields are unknown, mark them unknown.
Raw first. Classification later.
```

Short form:

```text
Hoard first. Sort later.
```
